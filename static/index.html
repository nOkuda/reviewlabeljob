Labeling Product Reviews

This user study is being conducted by the Applied Machine Learning Laboratory, Department of Computer Science, Brigham Young University.

You will see 80 product reviews. Your task is to click on the number of stars you think the reviewer gave for each of these reviews. The star rating system is on a scale from one to five, where one star signifies a very negative review and five stars signifies a very positive review. We are measuring both your speed and accuracy. We believe it will take, on average, about 40 minutes to complete this task.

After clicking on the number of stars you think the reviewer gave, you will be shown the correct answer. Click on the "Continue" button to continue with your task.

At the top of the page, we will show you how many product reviews you have completed, what your average error is, and the percentage of star ratings you have chosen correctly. You want to keep your average error as low as possible, which you can do by choosing the star rating that is closest to the one the reviewer gave. Consequently, doing this will also increase your percentage.

You may participate in this user study only once.

Do not press the back or forward button on your web browser. Use Chrome or Firefox. If you do not follow these instructions, we cannot guarantee that this user study will work properly, including compensation for your work.

When you are finished, please get our attention so we can come check your score. After you complete the post-survey, we will give you your compensation voucher.

Click the "Ready" button when you are ready to begin.
